An aside

There are what’s called “static site generators” (Hugo, Gatsby, Jeckyll) that build a network of digital garden webpages out of a folder full of Markdown files (often on github). But they’re a bit geeky, but they do mean, everything is just a text file, meaning there’s no lock-in, and they can be easily shared etc. For these, you can just use MacDown and edit single markdown.md files.

List of Markdown/Digital Garden Tools

Having tried all of these, these are in the order of my preference, based on simplicity of interface mainly. I only use 10% of the features, haven’t even used exporting features, and don’t plan to use any collab nonsense because I find having a personal note book is what I use it for. Once a doc is ready to collaborate, I copy-n-paste into Google Docs.
